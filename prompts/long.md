# Offboard me

I'm {{NAME}}, {{ROLE}} at {{COMPANY}}, and I'm leaving. Interview me
relentlessly about my knowledge transfer before I go, until we reach a clear,
complete picture of what needs to be handed off.

Focus on what's unrecoverable — what lives only in my head. Anything already in
a repo, wiki, doc, or someone else's hands: capture a one-line pointer to where
it lives and move on.

Walk down each branch of my work — active projects, ongoing responsibilities,
systems/tools only I know how to use, undocumented processes, key contacts and
relationships, open issues or landmines, and where things are stored —
resolving open threads one by one.

Ask the questions one at a time, waiting for my answer before continuing.
Asking multiple questions at once makes it easy to gloss over things.

For each area, push until we reach something concrete and actionable — not
"I'll write it up" but the actual detail here in the conversation, or an
explicit note that it needs to be documented separately, with an owner and
deadline. When I answer in generalities or say nothing comes to mind, switch to
episodes: the last incident I handled, the last time someone needed me while I
was away, the last shortcut I meant to fix later.

For everything that has to survive me, capture where it lives, who else knows
it, and whether those people are staying or also leaving. Never record
credentials themselves — only who holds them and where they're stored.

If a question can be answered from context I've already given (my role, team,
projects), don't re-ask it — confirm and move forward.

When we've covered everything — or the moment I say I want to stop — close
immediately with two things: a compact handoff summary of what we captured, in
my own words; and a checklist of everything that still needs to be documented
or handed off. Suggest an owner only if a person came up in our conversation —
otherwise mark it "owner needed." Then remind me to send the full transcript,
or at minimum your closing message, back to the colleague who sent me this
prompt.
