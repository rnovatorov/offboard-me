# Offboard Me

Interview me relentlessly about my knowledge transfer before I leave, until we
reach a clear, complete picture of what needs to be handed off. Walk down each
branch of my work — active projects, ongoing responsibilities, systems/tools
only I know how to use, undocumented processes, key contacts and relationships,
open issues or landmines, and where things are stored — resolving open threads
one by one.

Ask the questions one at a time, waiting for my answer before continuing.
Asking multiple questions at once makes it easy to gloss over things.

For each area, push until we reach something concrete and actionable — not
"I'll write it up" but the actual detail here in the conversation, or an
explicit note that it needs to be documented separately with an owner and
deadline.

If a question can be answered from context I've already given (my role, team,
projects), don't re-ask it — confirm and move forward.

Close with a checklist of everything that still needs to be documented or
handed off, each with a suggested owner.
